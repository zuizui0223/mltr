\begin{abstract}
Ecological prediction often reuses coarse state variables after turnover, rewiring, habitat replacement, or changes in the available interventions. Such transfer is valid only if structural change does not expose distinctions suppressed by the inherited macro-law. We formulate finite operational models in which a source macro-law is carried to a target system through a declared state relation and prove an exact portability criterion based on outputs, legal actions, and successors. When portability fails, the failure has a local operational witness and the inherited labels admit a unique coarsest source-relative exact refinement, which we interpret as the minimal repair required for valid prediction. The increase in repaired states, or equivalently in description length, defines a transport defect. For systems connected by several replacement histories, path coherence is necessary and sufficient for one route-independent carried repair; otherwise the minimum exact completion retains one immutable context for each distinct carried terminal map. A finite plant--pollinator turnover example shows how reuse of an inherited functional classification can reverse a restoration priority and how the theorem identifies the least additional ecological distinction needed to recover the correct decision.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Ecological models rarely retain every species identity, interaction, demographic state, and management history. Instead, they work with compressed variables: occupancy classes, functional guilds, successional stages, resilience categories, habitat states, or other macrostates chosen because they preserve the distinctions needed for a particular prediction. This compression is not merely convenient. It is what makes ecological forecasting, monitoring, and intervention design possible when the underlying system is too large, too heterogeneous, or too poorly observed to model in full detail.

The difficulty is that ecological systems do not remain structurally fixed. Species turn over, interaction networks rewire, habitats are replaced, and management itself changes the set of actions that can be applied. Under such change, a macrostate that was once sufficient can silently become insufficient. Two configurations previously treated as equivalent may respond differently to a newly available intervention, may permit different future actions, or may lead to different successor states. A model can therefore remain internally consistent and empirically familiar while no longer supporting the management conclusion for which it is being reused.

This creates a practical ecological question that is distinct from ordinary model fitting:
\begin{quote}
Given a macro-law already accepted for a source system, when can the same ecological classification be reused after structural change, and when reuse fails, what is the smallest additional distinction required to recover valid prediction?
\end{quote}
The question arises whenever a reduced model is transferred across sites, time periods, restoration scenarios, disturbance histories, or altered interaction networks. It is especially acute in adaptive management, because a change in the intervention repertoire can expose distinctions that were irrelevant under the original management regime. The relevant failure is structural rather than statistical: even with perfect knowledge of the declared systems, the inherited variable may no longer factor the target dynamics.

Several mature literatures address neighboring problems, but not this constrained ecological transfer problem. Coarse graining and lumpability ask whether a partition is exact within a specified dynamical or stochastic system. Bisimulation and partition-refinement methods identify behaviorally equivalent states and construct coarsest valid quotients. Transportability and domain adaptation ask whether causal or statistical conclusions transfer across environments. Resilience and regime-shift theory characterize persistence, thresholds, and qualitative change, while adaptive-management frameworks update decisions as evidence and system states change. These approaches are indispensable, but they do not by themselves answer whether an \emph{inherited} ecological macro-law remains exact after a declared structural replacement, nor do they identify the unique least repair constrained to preserve every inherited merge that remains valid.

We formulate that problem in a finite operational setting. A source macro-law is carried to a target system through a declared structural-change relation, which may connect non-nested state spaces. Exact portability is then tested against three ecological requirements: states sharing an inherited label must have the same current output, admit the same legal management actions, and lead under those actions to successors with the same inherited labels. This formulation makes the management repertoire part of the ecological model rather than an external afterthought. A classification can be adequate for observation alone yet fail once a new intervention is considered.

The framework yields four linked results. First, we give a necessary-and-sufficient operational portability criterion for unchanged reuse of the inherited law. Second, when portability fails, we show that failure has a finite local witness and that iterative refinement produces the unique coarsest exact target partition constrained to refine the carried source labels. This is the minimal ecological repair: it adds only distinctions forced by target outputs, legal actions, or successors. Third, the number of added states, or the corresponding increase in description length, defines a transport defect measuring the minimum loss of compression required to preserve inherited meaning. Fourth, when a target system can be reached through several declared replacement histories, we characterize exactly when one route-independent repair exists and when historical context must remain part of the predictive state.

The ecological value of these results is diagnostic rather than merely classificatory. The framework separates three cases that are often conflated in practice: an inherited variable remains valid; it fails but can be repaired by retaining one additional ecological distinction; or it becomes strongly path dependent and requires explicit historical context. In each case the output is a decision about model reuse, not simply a new partition. The plant--pollinator example developed below illustrates this distinction: a functional classification adequate under the original management regime recommends one restoration priority, whereas a target-only intervention exposes a hidden response channel and changes which site should be prioritized.

Our claims are deliberately limited. We do not propose a new generic partition-refinement algorithm, and we do not infer structural-change relations from data. Exactness is used as a benchmark for isolating structural failure before sampling error, parameter uncertainty, or approximate prediction are introduced. The contribution is instead a source-relative theory of ecological model reuse: a criterion for unchanged portability, a canonical minimal repair when portability fails, and a minimal account of historical context when replacement routes disagree.

\section{Operational framework}
A finite controlled ecological system is a tuple
\[
\mathcal{S}=(X,A,G,\delta,y),
\]
where $X$ is the state set, $A$ is the declared action set, $G$ is the legal finite action grammar, $\delta$ is the controlled transition rule, and $y$ is the observable output. A macro-law is a surjection $\pi:X\times G\to Q$.

\begin{definition}[Exact operational macro-law]
A macro-law is exact when states in the same macro-fiber have identical current outputs, identical legal-action rows, and successors carrying the same macro-label under every declared legal action.
\end{definition}

Structural change from a source system $\mathcal{S}_0$ to a target system $\mathcal{S}_1$ is represented by a declared total relation
\[
R\subseteq (X_0\times G_0)\times (X_1\times G_1).
\]
The source law induces a carried target partition $\pi_R$. Nested open composition is the special case in which the source embeds into a target with additional legal actions or interaction channels; ecological replacement is the general non-nested case.

\section{Results}
\subsection{Operational portability criterion}
\begin{theorem}[Operational portability]
For a finite exact source macro-law and a declared label-consistent structural-change relation, the carried target partition is exact if and only if current outputs, legal-action rows, and successor carried labels are constant inside every carried target fiber.
\end{theorem}

This theorem provides a direct diagnostic for whether a variable learned before structural change still supports every declared target prediction and intervention. It is source-relative: the target partition is tested as inherited, rather than replaced by an unrelated abstraction optimized only for the target.

\subsection{Local obstruction and unique coarsest repair}
\begin{proposition}[Local obstruction]
Failure of portability is witnessed by two target states in one inherited fiber that differ in current output, legal actions, or successor inherited labels under a declared action or finite legal future.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Unique coarsest source-relative repair]
Starting from the carried target partition and iteratively splitting fibers by output, legal-action row, and successor block yields a finite fixed point. The fixed point is exact and is the unique coarsest exact target partition refining the inherited labels.
\end{theorem}

The result does more than reject transfer. It identifies the minimum additional ecological distinction required to recover exact prediction while retaining every inherited merge that remains operationally valid. Standard partition-refinement machinery supplies the construction; the substantive claim is its source-relative minimality and uniqueness under structural transport.

\subsection{Transport defect}
\begin{definition}[Transport defect]
Let $Q_0$ be the source macrostate set and $Q_1^{\min}$ the unique repaired target macrostate set. Define
\[
\Delta_{\#}=|Q_1^{\min}|-|Q_0|,
\qquad
\Delta_{K}=\log_2|Q_1^{\min}|-\log_2|Q_0|.
\]
\end{definition}

These quantities measure the minimum burden of preserving inherited ecological meaning. In management terms, a small defect means that the old monitoring system can remain useful after adding a limited diagnostic distinction, whereas a large defect means that structural change has invalidated much of the former compression and therefore much of the old decision interface. In the verified accumulating family with $m$ independently exposed binary distinctions,
\[
|Q_0|=2,\qquad |Q_1^{\min}|=2^m+1,
\]
so $\Delta_{\#}=2^m-1$. This is a sharp finite witness, not a universal ecological scaling law. Its role is to show that apparently modest structural change can destroy most of the original compression when target operations independently expose previously merged coordinates.

\subsection{History coherence and minimum completion}
Consider a rooted directed acyclic graph of declared replacement relations. Every root-to-terminal path carries a source label map to the terminal system.

\begin{theorem}[History coherence]
A single route-independent carried partition, repaired partition, and transport defect exist if and only if every root-to-terminal path induces the same terminal carried label map.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Minimum history completion]
When terminal carried maps disagree, one immutable history mode per distinct carried terminal map is necessary and sufficient to preserve all declared histories. Relative exact refinement on the augmented system yields the unique coarsest exact history-aware interface.
\end{theorem}

Thus path identity is retained only when declared histories assign incompatible meanings to the same terminal configurations. The theorem does not infer historical pathways from field data; it characterizes the exact context required once those pathways and relations are declared.

\section{Worked ecological example: restoration priority after pollinator turnover}
Consider two degraded sites, A and B, containing the same focal plant and currently showing the same coarse pollination output. Under the source monitoring and management repertoire, the only relevant distinction is whether adequate pollination is maintained without intervention. Sites A and B therefore occupy the same functional macrostate: both are classified as ``pollination maintained,'' and their future labels coincide under the source actions. A third state represents failure of pollination service. In the verified finite witness, the inherited target labels are $(0,0,1)$.

A restoration program must choose which site to treat first. Under the inherited macro-law, A and B are predicted to respond identically, so the decision is made using treatment cost alone. Suppose competitor removal is cheaper at A. The inherited model therefore recommends A as the first restoration target. This is a genuine management conclusion produced by the coarse variable, not merely a descriptive grouping.

Structural change then alters the pollinator community. The original dominant pollinator is lost, but one target configuration retains access to a substitute guild whereas the other does not. Competitor removal is also introduced as a new legal action because managers can now open floral access around the focal plant. Under this target-only intervention, the two inherited states have different successors: at A, removal does not recover pollination because no substitute response channel is present; at B, removal releases access to the substitute guild and pollination is maintained. The inherited equality of A and B is therefore false for the decision now being made.

The portability criterion detects the failure without requiring the entire target model to be discarded. The local witness is the pair of target states carrying the same inherited label but producing different successor labels under competitor removal. Source-relative refinement splits only that inherited fiber, changing $(0,0,1)$ to $(0,1,2)$ while preserving all unaffected inherited merges. The transport defect is one additional macrostate. Ecologically, the repaired state variable adds exactly one field-relevant distinction: whether a site possesses a substitute-pollinator response channel that can be activated by the new intervention.

The management recommendation now reverses. Under the inherited law, A was preferred because treatment was cheaper and predicted outcomes were tied. Under the repaired law, B is preferred because only B retains the response channel needed for intervention success. The theorem therefore changes the decision from ``treat the cheaper apparently equivalent site'' to ``treat the site whose hidden interaction structure supports recovery.'' Figure~\ref{fig:local-split} summarizes the complete workflow from inherited aggregation to diagnostic failure, minimal repair, and revised restoration priority.

The same example also clarifies the historical result. Suppose the terminal community can be reached either by pollinator loss followed by substitute colonization or by habitat modification followed by loss of the original guild. If both routes carry the same terminal labels, the repaired management variable is route independent. If the routes assign incompatible inherited meanings to the same terminal configurations, no route-free label assignment exists. The minimum exact completion then retains one immutable mode for each distinct carried terminal map. Historical context is therefore added only when it changes the operational meaning of the terminal state.

\section{Discussion}
The plant--pollinator example shows why structural portability is an ecological decision problem rather than a purely formal property. Before turnover, the inherited functional state supports a defensible rule: if two sites are equivalent in expected response, prioritize the cheaper intervention. After turnover and expansion of the management repertoire, that same rule directs restoration toward the wrong site because the coarse state suppresses access to a substitute pollinator guild. The practical failure occurs even though the old labels remain interpretable and both sites may still look similar under routine monitoring.

The framework offers a staged response. First, it asks whether the inherited monitoring variable remains sufficient for the target actions. Second, when it does not, it returns a finite witness that can be translated into an ecological contrast to measure or record. Third, it identifies the least refinement needed to restore exact prediction. In the example, managers need not rebuild the model around every species and interaction; they need only distinguish sites by substitute-response capacity. This is the sense in which the repair is operationally minimal and potentially useful for monitoring design.

Transport defect then has a direct management interpretation. It measures how much additional state information must be retained before the old decision interface becomes valid again. A defect of one, as in the worked example, suggests that an existing monitoring program may be salvaged by adding one targeted variable. A large defect signals that the old aggregation has lost most of its decision value and that incremental patching may no longer be efficient. The defect is not itself a monetary cost or an empirical effect size, but it can organize comparisons among candidate monitoring schemes by quantifying the minimum structural information each must recover.

Historical coherence addresses a separate but common ecological difficulty. Restoration outcomes can depend on whether a community reached its present configuration through disturbance, colonization, management, or gradual turnover. The theory does not assume that history always matters. It gives a sharp criterion: history must remain in the predictive state only when distinct declared routes carry incompatible terminal meanings. This prevents both extremes---discarding path information automatically and retaining full histories when they do not alter the repaired interface.

The mathematical results support these ecological conclusions but do not replace empirical work. The framework is exact, finite, and conditional on declared systems, action grammars, outputs, and structural-change relations. It does not estimate those objects from field data, model imperfect detection, or quantify uncertainty in the transition rules. Nor does it establish that a proposed ecological classification is approximately adequate when exactness fails. Its immediate role is as a structural audit: before fitting or transferring a reduced model, investigators can ask whether the variables being reused are even capable of supporting the new interventions and forecasts.

Several extensions follow naturally, but they should be evaluated by ecological value rather than theorem count. Approximate portability would allow bounded predictive error; stochastic versions would connect repair to probabilistic transitions and imperfect observations; and data-estimated structural relations would permit empirical uncertainty to enter the audit. The worked example also suggests an applied workflow in which field measurements are chosen from the local obstruction: once competitor removal reveals substitute-response capacity as the missing distinction, monitoring can be redesigned specifically to estimate that trait or interaction channel.

\section{Methods and proof organization}
Proofs proceed by operational factorization and finite monotone partition refinement. The portability theorem follows by necessity and sufficiency of output, legal-row, and successor uniformity. Local obstruction is the contrapositive witness. Finite refinement terminates because every nontrivial update strictly refines a partition of a finite set; exactness holds at the fixed point, and any exact refinement of the carried labels must refine every intermediate partition, proving coarseness and uniqueness. History minimality follows because two paths can share one immutable mode exactly when their complete carried terminal maps agree.

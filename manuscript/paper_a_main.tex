\begin{abstract}
Ecological prediction often reuses coarse state variables after turnover, rewiring, habitat replacement, or changes in the available interventions. Such transfer is valid only if structural change does not expose distinctions suppressed by the inherited macro-law. We formulate finite operational models in which a source macro-law is carried to a target system through a declared state relation and prove an exact portability criterion based on outputs, legal actions, and successors. When portability fails, the failure has a local operational witness and the inherited labels admit a unique coarsest source-relative exact refinement, which we interpret as the minimal repair required for valid prediction. The increase in repaired states, or equivalently in description length, defines a transport defect. For systems connected by several replacement histories, path coherence is necessary and sufficient for one route-independent carried repair; otherwise the minimum exact completion retains one immutable context for each distinct carried terminal map. A finite plant--pollinator turnover example shows how a previously adequate functional classification can become invalid after a new management action is introduced and how the theorem identifies the least additional ecological distinction needed to restore exact prediction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Ecologists routinely compress high-dimensional configurations into occupancy classes, functional guilds, resilience categories, and other macrostates. These variables are useful because they suppress distinctions that do not matter for the predictions or interventions under consideration. The difficulty is that ecological structure is not fixed. Species turnover, interaction rewiring, habitat replacement, and management innovation can alter both the state space and the repertoire of legal future actions. A classification that was exact in the source system may therefore cease to be exact in the target system even when its labels retain an apparently natural ecological interpretation.

Existing theories of coarse graining, lumpability, abstraction, and bisimulation provide mature criteria for exact aggregation within a fixed system. Transportability studies whether conclusions carry across environments. The present problem combines these concerns but is not exhausted by either: given an already accepted ecological macro-law and a declared structural change, when does that same law remain exact, and if it fails, what is the unique least change that restores exact prediction while preserving its source meaning?

We answer this question in a finite operational setting. A structural-change relation carries source semantics to target configurations, including non-nested changes in which source and target state spaces differ. Our contribution is organized around four result packages. First, an operational portability criterion characterizes unchanged transport by uniformity of outputs, legal-action rows, and target successors within inherited fibers. Second, any failure has a local operational witness, and iterative refinement yields the unique coarsest exact target partition constrained to refine the inherited labels. Third, the additional states or bits required by this repair define a transport defect. Fourth, path coherence characterizes when several replacement histories share one repair; when they do not, the minimum exact completion retains one history mode for each distinct carried terminal map.

The ecological conclusion is deliberately sharper than a generic warning that transfer may fail. The framework distinguishes unchanged validity from structural invalidation and, in the latter case, identifies the least additional ecological distinction that must be retained. Exactness is used here as a benchmark that separates structural failure from statistical estimation error; inference of the source--target relation and approximate or stochastic extensions remain outside the theorem claims.

\section{Operational framework}
A finite controlled ecological system is a tuple
\[
\mathcal{S}=(X,A,G,\delta,y),
\]
where $X$ is the state set, $A$ is the declared action set, $G$ is the legal finite action grammar, $\delta$ is the controlled transition rule, and $y$ is the observable output. A macro-law is a surjection $\pi:X\times G\to Q$.

\begin{definition}[Exact operational macro-law]
A macro-law is exact when states in the same macro-fiber have identical current outputs, identical legal-action rows, and successors carrying the same macro-label under every declared legal action.
\end{definition}

Structural change from a source system $\mathcal{S}_0$ to a target system $\mathcal{S}_1$ is represented by a declared total relation
\[
R\subseteq (X_0\times G_0)\times (X_1\times G_1).
\]
The source law induces a carried target partition $\pi_R$. Nested open composition is the special case in which the source embeds into a target with additional legal actions or interaction channels; ecological replacement is the general non-nested case.

\section{Results}
\subsection{Operational portability criterion}
\begin{theorem}[Operational portability]
For a finite exact source macro-law and a declared label-consistent structural-change relation, the carried target partition is exact if and only if current outputs, legal-action rows, and successor carried labels are constant inside every carried target fiber.
\end{theorem}

This theorem provides a direct diagnostic for whether a variable learned before structural change still supports every declared target prediction and intervention. It is source-relative: the target partition is tested as inherited, rather than replaced by an unrelated abstraction optimized only for the target.

\subsection{Local obstruction and unique coarsest repair}
\begin{proposition}[Local obstruction]
Failure of portability is witnessed by two target states in one inherited fiber that differ in current output, legal actions, or successor inherited labels under a declared action or finite legal future.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Unique coarsest source-relative repair]
Starting from the carried target partition and iteratively splitting fibers by output, legal-action row, and successor block yields a finite fixed point. The fixed point is exact and is the unique coarsest exact target partition refining the inherited labels.
\end{theorem}

The result does more than reject transfer. It identifies the minimum additional ecological distinction required to recover exact prediction while retaining every inherited merge that remains operationally valid. Standard partition-refinement machinery supplies the construction; the substantive claim is its source-relative minimality and uniqueness under structural transport.

\subsection{Transport defect}
\begin{definition}[Transport defect]
Let $Q_0$ be the source macrostate set and $Q_1^{\min}$ the unique repaired target macrostate set. Define
\[
\Delta_{\#}=|Q_1^{\min}|-|Q_0|,
\qquad
\Delta_{K}=\log_2|Q_1^{\min}|-\log_2|Q_0|.
\]
\end{definition}

These quantities measure the minimum burden of preserving inherited ecological meaning. In the verified accumulating family with $m$ independently exposed binary distinctions,
\[
|Q_0|=2,\qquad |Q_1^{\min}|=2^m+1,
\]
so $\Delta_{\#}=2^m-1$. This is a sharp finite witness, not a universal ecological scaling law. Its role is to show that apparently modest structural change can destroy most of the original compression when target operations independently expose previously merged coordinates.

\subsection{History coherence and minimum completion}
Consider a rooted directed acyclic graph of declared replacement relations. Every root-to-terminal path carries a source label map to the terminal system.

\begin{theorem}[History coherence]
A single route-independent carried partition, repaired partition, and transport defect exist if and only if every root-to-terminal path induces the same terminal carried label map.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Minimum history completion]
When terminal carried maps disagree, one immutable history mode per distinct carried terminal map is necessary and sufficient to preserve all declared histories. Relative exact refinement on the augmented system yields the unique coarsest exact history-aware interface.
\end{theorem}

Thus path identity is retained only when declared histories assign incompatible meanings to the same terminal configurations. The theorem does not infer historical pathways from field data; it characterizes the exact context required once those pathways and relations are declared.

\section{Worked ecological example}
Consider a source plant--pollinator community in which two microstates share the same focal-plant output and have identical futures under the source management repertoire. An exact functional macro-law therefore merges them. Turnover carries the labels to a target community in which a pollinator exclusion or competitor-removal action becomes legal. The two inherited states now respond differently: one retains pollination through a substitute guild, whereas the other loses the interaction channel.

The target-only action is a local obstruction, so unchanged portability is rejected. The source-relative refinement splits exactly the inherited fiber containing these two states and leaves all other inherited fibers unchanged. In the finite witness the carried labels $(0,0,1)$ become $(0,1,2)$, giving one additional state and the corresponding positive bit defect. The repaired variable has a direct ecological meaning: it retains whether an apparently equivalent functional state possesses the substitute response channel exposed by the new intervention.

Now suppose the same terminal community can be reached through two loss--colonization routes. If both routes carry the same terminal labels, the repaired law is route independent. If one route carries $(0,1)$ and the other $(1,0)$, no route-free inherited label assignment exists. Two history modes are then necessary and sufficient, and refinement on the augmented system gives the exact history-aware predictive state. The example therefore uses every main result: portability, local failure, minimal repair, defect, coherence, and minimum historical completion.

\section{Discussion}
The main conclusion is that ecological macro-laws are operational objects rather than timeless partitions. Their validity depends on the outputs, interventions, and legal futures against which states are compared. Structural change can therefore invalidate an inherited variable without changing its verbal label or its fit to currently observed states.

The central contribution is the unique coarsest source-relative repair. Coarse graining and bisimulation characterize behavioral equivalence, and partition refinement is established machinery. What is added here is a transport problem in which source semantics are carried through a possibly non-nested relation, tested against the target grammar, and repaired under the constraint that inherited distinctions are preserved. This constraint makes the result different from discarding the source law and computing a new target abstraction from scratch.

Transport defect quantifies the price of preserving that inherited meaning. It separates mild change requiring one additional state from structural change that destroys most of the old compression. Historical coherence adds a second decision: one repaired management model is valid across all declared routes exactly when those routes carry the same terminal semantics; otherwise route identity must remain in the predictive state, but only at the minimum cardinality required by the distinct maps.

The theory is exact, finite, and conditional on declared systems, grammars, and structural-change relations. It does not estimate those objects from data, establish empirical exactness, or cover stochastic and approximate repair. These limitations are intentional. Exactness provides a benchmark for identifying structural failure before observation error, model misspecification, or approximate prediction are introduced. Approximate, stochastic, and data-estimated variants are important next steps, but they should be developed as complete results rather than appended as additional theorem families.

\section{Methods and proof organization}
Proofs proceed by operational factorization and finite monotone partition refinement. The portability theorem follows by necessity and sufficiency of output, legal-row, and successor uniformity. Local obstruction is the contrapositive witness. Finite refinement terminates because every nontrivial update strictly refines a partition of a finite set; exactness holds at the fixed point, and any exact refinement of the carried labels must refine every intermediate partition, proving coarseness and uniqueness. History minimality follows because two paths can share one immutable mode exactly when their complete carried terminal maps agree. Detailed proof dependencies and reproducibility commands are maintained in the manuscript repository and supplement.

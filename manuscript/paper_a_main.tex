\begin{abstract}
Ecological models compress many configurations into functional macrostates, yet community turnover, novel interactions, and management interventions can expose distinctions hidden by the inherited law. We give an exact finite criterion for portability after structural change, a finite local obstruction when portability fails, and the unique coarsest source-relative exact repair. The increase in states or bits defines a transport defect. A sharp family shows that repair burden can grow rapidly as independent target distinctions become operational. Across replacement histories, route-independent repair exists exactly when all routes carry the same terminal label map; otherwise a minimum finite history context is necessary and sufficient. A plant--pollinator turnover example illustrates the theory.
\end{abstract}
